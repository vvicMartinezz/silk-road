## Trade Networks and the Silk Road
The interactions that occurred between Silla and other cultures around it were not only based on its reputation but also actively participated in frade networks. The Silk Riad is oen of the major networks that connected East Asia to Central Asia, the Middle East, and Europe. Most of the maps of the ancient trade networks do not show the role played by Korea but recent research shows that there were interactions through such routes.

The interactions that accurred between Silla and other cultures around it were not according to Sun Woong Bang "the culture of ancient Korea had never been a totally isolated entity… it had a constant flow of interaction with various cultures" (Bang 2016, 25). He also talks about how Koreans how traded their goods like silk, glass and jewelery from the fourth century to the tenth century. These goods have no origin in Korea and show were obtained through trade with foreihn societies which taught Koreas to foreign technology. 

The trade of goods resulted in an exchange of other cultural aspects. People like merchants whho were traders moved from one region to another showing ways they exchanged ideas.

## The Golden Earrings and Artistic Exchange
Archaeological discoveries also serve as evidence of cultural interaction, especially because of artifacrs with evident influence frim another culture. The artifacts that serve as a great example of such interactions like the earrings found in Gyeongju. What makes these special is that it differs significantly in design and material used from other objects created in Silla. 

Specifically, Bang also talks about how these objects from the Silla are almost always covered with gold sheets and other unknown genstones they couldn't idenify. The presence of the beaded matterials used for these earrings, together with the way it is designed, suggests a connection to both Central Asia and the Byzantine Empire. The earrings can be classified as either impirted or greatly influenced by another culture. 

This show that the exchange of cultures had a direct impact on artistic activity in Silla. Instead of staying completely cut off from the rest of the world, Silla artists began implementing foreign elements into their art.

## Conclusion
Well evidence from the Silla Kingdom proves that cultural exchange had an impact on ancient Korea. Being a country known for its abundance in gold, Silla was well known to many other civilizations. The involvement of Silla in trade relations through such routes as the Silk Road demonstrates the impact of international comminication on the civilization. Examples of cultural exchange in Silla include the mixing of artistic styles seen in such goods as the golden earrings or other common symbols.

Silla, can nor be considered a separate and self sufficient civilization, but a complex system that underwent influence from both inernal and exernal factors. Curtual exchange was one of those aspects that defined the Korean history.
